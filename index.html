Nelson Mandela

Nelson Mandela, Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela in full name, was born in July 18th, 1918, South Africa. He was a lawyer and political activists fighting for freedom and equality.

Background

Nelson Mandela, Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela in the full name, was born on July 18th, 1918, in South Africa. He is the first black president of South Africa. He is a son of Henry Mandela, the chief of Madiba clan. Mandela’s father died when he was age 7, so his uncle took him in. He attended the University of Fort Hare, called South African Native College at that time. In 1941, he got expelled from the University of Fort Hare because he lead a group of students on a political strike. Two years later, he enrolled at the University of Witwatersrand and studied law. He was qualified for a lawyer after he passed the qualification test. He later joined ANC(African National Council) in 1944.

What he tried to change

Nelson Mandela grew up learning about racial discrimination in South Africa. He was against apartheid, legislation on segregation of different races. He fought for freedom and equality for his country. This wasn’t easy though. NP, National Party, kept resisting and blocking Mandella. As Mandella started gathering more people, National Party kept enforcing more and more laws against ANC. He was banned from travel, association, and speech in 1992. And he was arrested twice in 1956 and 1963.

Strategies used

Nelson was inspired by Mahatma Gandhi’s non-violent protests. In 1952, he did a defiance act against apartheid and lawyered black people, specializing in cases on apartheid legislation. He also drafted a “Freedom Charter”, a document containing principals of South African Congress Alliance. However, he got arrested in 1956 because of an enforced law on protesters against apartheid. On top of that, the shooting of unarmed black citizens made Nelson put down non-violent protests and move on to violent styles. He went to Algeria to practice guerrilla warfare and sabotage in 1962. He returned to South Africa a year later and got arrested shortly. Nelson almost got a death penalty for firearms possession and uprising, but he nearly evaded it and got a life sentence instead. While he was imprisoned, he started to gather crowds that support him. In 1990, he was released by the South African government. Four years later, in 1994, he was elected as South Africa’s president in South Africa’s first democratic election. He immediately started making changes to South Africa by creating TRC, Truth and Reconciliation Commission. It was created to investigate human rights violations. Also, he improved black people’s life by providing them housing, education, and economic development.

Results of his work

Nelson Mandela successively ended apartheid in South Africa. He is still admired by people around the world and is a symbol of dedication, sacrifice, and courage. He resigned from his presidential career in 1997 and retired from politics. However, he remained a strong international figure. After his retirement, he founded “the Elders”, a group consisting of international leaders. Also, he found the Nelson Mandela Foundation in 1999. He also became a vocal advocate for AIDS. 11 years later, Mandela's day was created on his birthday to honor his legacy.

What does history say?

The world is very positive about Nelson Mandela and thinks he is a symbol of dedication, sacrifice, and courage. The critics say he contributed a lot to society with his actions and will inspire other leaders. Many organizations took up his HIV/AIDS advocation and there also are organizations that took up his legacy in equality and freedom.
